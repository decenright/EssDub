National Art Gallery

A little history

On the 30th of January 1864, the Earl of Carlisle officially opened the National Gallery of Ireland on Merrion Square ten years after it was first founded. Housing an impressive collection of Irish painting with notable paintings from both the Italian baroque and Dutch masters. In 1866 an annual purchase grant was put in place and by the beginning of the 1890's space was becoming limited. In 1897 the Dowager Countess of Milltown intended to donate an extensive collection of 20,000 paintings from Russborough House to the gallery.
